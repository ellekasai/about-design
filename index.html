I was a sales person

I was born in Phillippines and raised in Kanagawa, Japan. After graduating from Gakushuin Women's College with a Marketing degree in 2012, I started working at a payment startup in Tokyo as a sales associate.

But I've always loved to express myself and be creative, and I wasn't quite satisfied with my job. So after a year, I decided to pursue a completely different career in UI design.

I didn't go to art or design school, so I thought it'll be hard to compete with other designers on graphic design. That's why I decided to study HTML & CSS first. This was a great decision because I fell in love with coding immediately.

Shortly afterwards, I left my job. I stayed with my boyfriend in Silicon Valley and learned coding and design from him.

Eventually I came back to Japan, and in Fall 2013, I became a junior product designer at BizReach. BizReach is Japan’s top job site exclusive to business executives.

Mainly I worked on front-end design and coding, but sometimes I was an instructor for engineer onboarding too. I taught basics of HTML, CSS, and Git to approx. 15 new employees. I prepared my own curriculum and teaching materials.

In Fall 2014, I left my job in Tokyo and moved to Vancouver, Canada for new challenges.

Attending conferences

My first experience of a conference was CSS Conf US in 2013. I visited Omni resort hotel in Frolida. When I heard talks from speakers, I was fascinated by their valuable knowledge of design and code. I became interested in these community.

Ater about a year later, I attended CSS Conf EU, Smashing Conf Whistler, and Style & Class Conf Vancouver. These time, I tried chatting with attendees and sharing knowledge each other. Attendees were very kind and friendly, so I could making friends easily. If you see me at a conference, please say hi to me. I'd love to chat with you too!
